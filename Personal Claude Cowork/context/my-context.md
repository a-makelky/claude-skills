## Personal Brand Context

### Background

Aaron Makelky spent 16 years as a high school teacher and football coach in Wyoming before moving to San Francisco and transitioning into tech. This career pivot is central to his personal brand and provides the contrast that runs through everything he creates: rural to urban, education to tech, skeptic to builder.

### Content Niche

AI and tech tools made practical for people who don't code. The core thesis: boring, practical AI use cases matter more than impressive demos. Save someone 45 minutes a week rather than blow their mind for 30 seconds.

### Audience

Non-technical professionals curious about AI, creators building personal brands, people navigating career pivots, and anyone tired of AI hype who wants to know what actually works.

### Content Accompaniments

Posts usually include either a vertical video or a static graphic. Static graphics are typically screenshots (tool interfaces, results, conversations, etc.).

### Content Pipeline

**Sources → Notion → Content**

1. **Inputs:** Day job meetings, side projects, conversations, other creators' posts, conferences/workshops, personal experiences
2. **Capture:** Meeting notes and ideas stored in Notion
3. **Processing:** Surface patterns and themes from captured notes (this is where Claude helps most)
4. **Creation:** Draft using VOICE.md → format for platform → audit for AI tells
5. **Publishing:** Notion content calendar → Post-Bridge scheduling or native posting

### Ongoing Need: Meeting Notes → Content Ideas

Aaron records meeting notes in Notion and wants a system for surfacing patterns, recurring themes, and content-worthy moments from those notes. The goal is to find content in what he's already doing rather than manufacturing topics from scratch. This pipeline is a priority for the workspace.
