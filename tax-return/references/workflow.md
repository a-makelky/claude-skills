# Tax Return Workflow

Use this as the detailed operating playbook when the user wants help organizing and preparing a tax return.

## 1. Create one tax workspace

Create one folder for the tax year. Keep the raw documents, workpapers, and upload-ready copies in that workspace.

Use one consistent structure:

- `00_INBOX`
- `01_PERSONAL_1040`
- `02_<ENTITY_1>`
- `03_<ENTITY_2>`
- `04_SHARED_WORKPAPERS`
- `99_REVIEW_HOLD`

Add subfolders only when they map cleanly to filing needs, such as:

- wages and W-2s
- health and HSA
- mortgage and property
- investment and interest
- income
- expenses
- bank and card statements
- mileage
- bookings and occupancy
- workpapers

## 2. Use intake first

Route all newly discovered files into `00_INBOX`.

Do not force perfect classification at the moment of arrival. Process inbox items in batches:

1. identify the file
2. rename it
3. classify it
4. move it
5. note any questions or conflicts

## 3. Apply one naming convention

Use:

`[Year] - [Entity] - [Document Type] - [Detail].ext`

Examples:

- `2025 - Personal - W2 - Employer - Aaron.pdf`
- `2025 - Business - 1099-NEC - Client.pdf`
- `2025 - Rental - Mortgage Interest - Lender.pdf`
- `2025 - Business - Checking Activity Export.csv`

Prefer names that are useful without opening the file.

## 4. Separate by filing bucket

Organize around how the item will be used on the return, not where it came from.

Typical buckets:

- personal wage forms
- personal investment and interest forms
- health and HSA forms
- mortgage and property forms
- business income
- business expenses
- rental income
- rental occupancy
- rental mortgage, tax, insurance, and expenses
- shared exports and reconciliation files

## 5. Lock down the facts before totals

Before reconciling numbers, establish:

- who owns each entity
- what each entity does
- what filing treatment is assumed or still open
- which accounts belong to which entity
- which property belongs to which activity
- whether a form was issued to an SSN or EIN
- whether mixed-use logs can be reliably split

Document unresolved treatment questions for CPA review.

## 6. Reconcile income first

For each person and entity:

1. list the forms on file
2. compare them to deposits and earnings summaries
3. identify income with no form
4. identify forms that conflict with actual deposits
5. document which figure is being used in the workpapers and why

Do not silently overwrite conflicts. Preserve the conflict and explain it.

## 7. Reconcile expenses second

Normalize expenses after income.

Watch for:

- transfers
- owner contributions
- owner draws
- credit-card payments versus underlying card charges
- reimbursed items
- off-account expenses paid from personal cards
- major purchases that need invoice support

Use workpapers to separate the raw transactions from the tax-facing categories.

## 8. Keep rental support explicit

For rental activity, document:

- booked nights or days rented
- turnover dates
- personal-use days
- platform summaries
- mortgage forms
- taxes
- insurance
- HOA
- utilities
- repairs and supplies

Even when bank statements are enough to prepare the return, occupancy and property mapping are still worth documenting.

## 9. Split mixed logs and exports

If a mileage or expense export mixes multiple activities:

- preserve the raw export in shared workpapers
- split it into entity-specific files
- document the mapping rules
- note any manual entries or exclusions

This is especially important for mileage logs and bank exports that include both personal and business activity.

## 10. Use a manual transcription log

When PDFs are image-only or hard to OCR, create a manual read queue.

For each item, log:

- file name
- payer or lender
- taxpayer
- relevant boxes
- exact values
- uncertainty or follow-up notes

Feed the confirmed values into the workpapers after they are manually checked.

## 11. Use review-hold aggressively

Move items to `99_REVIEW_HOLD` when they are:

- duplicates
- wrong-year documents
- ambiguous
- non-tax reference material
- unsupported deductions
- alternate scans

Keep the filing packet clean by refusing to force uncertain items into the main folders.

## 12. Maintain one master memo

At the end, condense the useful notes into one CPA-facing memo that explains:

- what is in the packet
- who and what entities are involved
- what is already reconciled
- what facts are known
- what still needs CPA judgment
- what follow-up items matter only if they exist

Do not leave multiple overlapping summary documents in the final packet.

## 13. Build the upload package last

Create a clean upload package only after the workspace is stable.

Typical rules:

- exclude `00_INBOX`
- exclude `99_REVIEW_HOLD`
- convert `.md` notes into `.docx`
- convert `.csv` workpapers into `.xlsx`
- leave PDFs and images unchanged
- preserve the original workspace files

## 14. Improve the next tax year during the current one

Recommend these habits:

- save tax forms as they arrive
- export business and rental transactions quarterly
- save invoices for major purchases immediately
- keep a note for unusual transactions
- keep personal-card business or rental charges in a small side folder
- download platform year-end summaries before access changes

The skill should favor small, repeatable cleanup steps over a large scramble at filing time.
