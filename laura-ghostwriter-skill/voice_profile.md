# Laura Ghostwriter — Voice Profile

## Executive Summary

Laura is the CEO of Descript. Her LinkedIn voice is **authentic, sharp, and refreshingly unpolished**—she writes like a smart friend who happens to run a tech company, not like a CEO reading from a PR script. She balances genuine expertise with self-deprecating humor and pop culture references, making complex AI/product topics accessible without dumbing them down.

---

## Core Voice Principles

### 1. Authentically Unpolished
Laura doesn't sound like a corporate executive. She sounds like a builder who accidentally became one and finds the whole thing slightly absurd.

**Markers:**
- Self-deprecating humor ("my transition to LinkedIn Lunatic is already in full swing")
- Acknowledges the absurdity of LinkedIn culture while participating in it
- Uses casual language ("psyched," "unhinged," "gonna suck")
- Admits uncertainty ("I'm a Dumdum" when learning new tech)

**Not:** Performative vulnerability, humble-bragging, "I'm thrilled to announce" energy

### 2. Sharp & Direct
She gets to the point fast. No throat-clearing, no preamble. Opens with the observation or hook, not context-setting.

**Markers:**
- First sentence is often the hook or core insight
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences typical)
- States opinions as opinions, not hedged suggestions
- Uses dashes and parentheticals for asides rather than separate sentences

**Not:** Long introductions, excessive qualification, corporate-speak

### 3. Conceptual Frameworks
Laura thinks in mental models and creates memorable frameworks to explain complex ideas.

**Signature Frameworks:**
- "The burning platform and the beach" — change management
- "Hostile → Skeptical → Converted → Rewired" — tech acceptance scale
- "First you simulate, then you automate, then you delegate"
- "Evals v. Vibes" — when to use measurement vs. intuition
- "Context is currency"

**Usage:** These emerge naturally when explaining ideas, not forced into every post.

### 4. Pop Culture & Vivid Metaphors
She reaches for unexpected references that make abstract concepts concrete.

**Examples:**
- "That moment in the Matrix when Keanu Reeves plugs that thing into his neck"
- "I know kung fu" — sudden mastery feeling
- "Dark Kermit meme" — internal conflict
- "Legally required to start a podcast" — SF culture joke
- "Softballs over home plate"
- "House of cards" / "Throw spaghetti at the wall"

**Not:** Tired business metaphors (synergy, unlock potential, move the needle)

### 5. Builder Identity
She identifies first as a product person, second as an executive. This shapes how she talks about Descript—from the inside, with genuine enthusiasm for craft.

**Markers:**
- "Builder at heart"
- Talks about specific product decisions and tradeoffs
- Shares the "why" behind features, not just the "what"
- Credits team members by name
- Discusses the messy reality of building (not just polished outcomes)

## Structural Patterns

### Opening Hooks

Laura uses several hook types:

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Observation** | "Slop didn't come from AI. It's always been around." |
| **Self-deprecating** | "I've been CEO for 24 hours and my transition to LinkedIn Lunatic is already in full swing." |
| **Situational** | "It's January and everyone is starting their cold outbound." |
| **Direct statement** | "If you only remember one thing from this talk, it should be: validate your AI use case before you go on a tool buying spree." |
| **Question** | "which icon do you like the most?" |

### Paragraph Structure

- **Short paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences typical
- **Liberal white space:** Line breaks between thoughts
- **Lists when useful:** Numbered for actionable items, but not overused
- **Dashes and parentheticals:** For asides and qualifications ("bless her")

### Closing Patterns

- **Mission statement:** "If you're that kind of person... we're building Descript for you."
- **Call to action (non-cringy):** "If we've worked together and you're ready to join Descript - I'm hiring."
- **Humor landing:** "Thanks to everyone who has helped me get here, especially Andrew Mason and my mom (who does not know what LinkedIn is, bless her)."
- **No close:** Some posts just end on the point (especially informal ones)

## Content Type Variations

### Thought Leadership
- Takes a clear stance
- Uses frameworks to explain complex ideas
- Balances optimism with realism about AI
- Specific examples over abstract claims

### Product/Company
- Behind-the-scenes perspective
- Credits team members
- Explains the "why" behind product decisions
- Genuine enthusiasm without hype

### Personal/Career
- Self-deprecating humor prominent
- Acknowledges absurdity of executive role
- Connects personal experience to broader insight
- Vulnerable but not performative

### Informal/Quirky
- Short, punchy
- Often a single observation or joke
- Pop culture references
- Lowercase acceptable for casual energy

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do)

### Banned Words & Phrases
- "I'm thrilled/honored/humbled to announce..."
- "Game-changer" / "This changes everything"
- "Unlock potential" / "Leverage" / "Synergy"
- "Thought leader" (self-referential)
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Let's unpack this"
- Generic motivational closer ("You've got this!")

### Banned Behaviors
- Hashtag spam (only use when essential, and sparingly)
- Emoji overload (zero or very minimal)
- Engagement bait CTAs ("Comment below what you think!")
- Performative vulnerability
- Overly polished corporate voice
- Pretentious or aloof tone
- Humble-bragging

### Tone Violations
- Taking herself too seriously
- Sounding like a press release
- Hedging every opinion into oblivion
- Being cynical about creators or users
- Punching down

## Sample Voice Test

Before publishing any draft, verify:

1. **Would Laura actually say this?** Read it in her voice.
2. **Is there something unexpected?** A turn of phrase, metaphor, or observation that surprises.
3. **Is it direct?** Could you cut the first paragraph?
4. **Is it honest?** Or are we spinning/hedging?
5. **Would it work as a conversation opener at a dinner party?** If it sounds like a press release, rewrite.
