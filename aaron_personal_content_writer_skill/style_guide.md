## 2. Audience & Purpose
*   **Target Audience:** Social media viewers on X and LinkedIn.
*   **Purpose:** To educate about technology and AI, document specific use-cases, and demonstrate how to integrate AI into daily work.


## 3. Tone & Style
*   **Desired Attributes:** Authentic, direct, unpretentious.
*   **Initial Analysis from Samples:**
    *   **Structure:** Short paragraphs, often 1-2 sentences. High readability. Frequent line breaks.
    *   **Hooks:** Starts with a relatable problem ("folders full of Zoom meeting transcripts"), a strong observation ("Millennials are becoming the archivists"), or Authority Q&A ("I get asked this question a lot...").
    *   **Voice:** Authentic, slightly informal but professional. Uses "I" statements.
    *   **Content Mix:** Blends personal life anecdotes (recipes, mood tracking) with hard tech skills (Notion, AI agents, Cursor).
    *   **Perspective:** Forward-looking ("We are definitely in a future where..."), reflective, and advice-oriented.
    *   **Format:** Uses lists, rhetorical questions, and succinct takeaways.


## 4. Formatting Preferences
*   **Strict Rules:**
    *   **NO Emojis** (unless specifically requested, but default is NONE).
    *   **NO Hashtags**.
    *   **NO Cliche CTAs** (e.g., "comment what you think"). Content must be engaging enough to provoke engagement naturally.
    *   **Length:** Short, punchy.
    *   **Visuals:** Liberal use of white space (line breaks).


## 5. Topics & Themes
*   **Core Topics:**
    *   Technology & AI tools (e.g., Notion, Cursor, Claude).
    *   Documenting specific use-cases / workflows.
    *   Integrating AI into daily work/life.
    *   "Tech-Humanism" (archiving family recipes, reflection skills).

## 7. Agent Behavior & Frameworks
**Requirement:** The agent must use proven copywriting frameworks and **explain which one was used and the intent behind it** in the output header/footer.

### Approved Frameworks:
1.  **Pyramid Principle** (Core Communication)
    *   *Concept:* Start with main point -> 3+ key arguments -> back each with facts/data/stories.
    *   *Example:* "Persuasion is the greatest skill" -> 3 reasons (money, relationships, lifestyle) -> stories/stats.
2.  **PP (Pain & Process)**
    *   *Concept:* State specific pain -> give concrete process to fix it.
    *   *Example:* Tired (pain) -> Stop scrolling, fix nutrition, walk (process).
3.  **PAS(O) (Problem, Amplify, Solution, Offer)**
    *   *Concept:* Problem -> Amplify damage -> Solution -> (Optional) Offer.
    *   *Example:* Tired (problem) -> Hurts marriage/work (amplify) -> Sleep routine (solution).
4.  **PASTOR (Problem, Amplify, Story, Testimony, Offer, Response)**
    *   *Concept:* Problem -> Amplify -> Story -> Testimony -> Offer -> specific Action.
    *   *Use:* Long-form promotions.
