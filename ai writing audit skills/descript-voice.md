# Descript Brand Voice

Descript speaks in a way that's **human**, **realistic**, and **subversive**. Being realistic often feels authentically human, which itself is subversive for a brand.

---

## Core characteristics

### Human

Write like a person, not a brand. Never like a SaaS company.

- Write like you talk
- Avoid jargon, memes, pop-culture references, hyper-online language
- Speak directly to readers, but don't pretend they're friends
- Never be presumptuous ("you love Descript," "your masterpiece")

### Realistic

Be honest, empathetic, and self-aware.

- Say it like a real person: conversational, casual, no faux enthusiasm
- Say it like it really is, not how we want it to be
- Avoid positive spin / trying to make people feel fake-good
- When we know how creators feel about something, call it out
- Acknowledge our own imperfections and limitations

### Subversive

Undermine expectations for brand communications.

- **Surprising**: what you wouldn't expect from a brand
- **Uncomfortable**: say the parts most brands steer clear of
- **Inobvious**: reach for the moment that makes them stop and think, not the easy punch line
- **Absurd**: drive toward the weird thing everyone takes for granted

---

## Never be

- Mean, condescending, dismissive, or hurtful
- Punching down (only mock big shots like MrBeast, and even then be faux-reverent)
- Cynical (don't assume the worst)
- Discouraging (realistic but hopeful)
- Conspicuously authentic (don't try to sound like a cool kid)

---

## Voice test questions

When reviewing copy, ask:

1. Would you least expect a brand to say this?
2. Is it surprising?
3. Is it funny in an inobvious way?
4. Does it rely on memes or pop culture? (Kill it if so)
5. Is it punching down or mean-spirited?
6. Will it make someone feel worse?

---

## Example patterns

**Good**: "Nobody really knows how, but like all of these platforms there are certain things you can do to give yourself a fighting chance."

**Good**: "Don't worry, we're not doing that again."

**Good**: "We'll do our best to answer (on some of this stuff, we have no idea either)."

**Good**: "Buying an expensive camera is like opening a chain of burger joints — it won't make you a YouTube star, but you should do it anyway."

**Bad**: "Your masterpiece awaits!"

**Bad**: "We're thrilled to announce..."

**Bad**: "Unlock your creative potential"

**Bad**: Any fake enthusiasm or unrealistic promises about creative success
